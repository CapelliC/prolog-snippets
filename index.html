About lifter, I can't resist...

here is part of a comment from SWI-Prolog mailing list [https://lists.iai.uni-bonn.de/pipermail/swi-prolog/2013/011893.html], explaining what lifter is.

From the very first lifter' users:

With lifter, the clause

longer(A,B) :-
    length(A,ยบ) > length(B,ยบ).

is expanded to

longer(A, B) :-
    length(A, C),
    length(B, D),
    C > D.

I like that this approach satisfies the desire for expressions that "evaluate in place" but keeps the flexibility and potent, multi-directional functionality of prolog relations.
